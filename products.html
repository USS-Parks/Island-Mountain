Hardware Integrity

Why Professional-Grade GPUs

The NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell Server Edition cards in Island Mountain systems are not the same hardware you find in gaming PCs or consumer AI builds. They are professional server GPUs, purpose-built for always-on deployment in a rack, and that distinction matters for regulated buyers.

They carry ECC GDDR7 memory, which detects and corrects single-bit memory errors automatically. For workloads where a silent data flip is unacceptable - clinical, legal, financial, and defense inference - error-correcting memory is the difference between a result you can stand behind and one you cannot. Consumer cards do not have it.

They are built for the server room: passive cooling designed for front-to-back rack airflow, PCIe Gen 5 for maximum host throughput, and professional ISV-certified drivers with a validated, long-lived support lifecycle. They are rated for a continuous, always-on duty cycle rather than the bursty gaming workload a consumer card is designed around. Every card also ships with full procurement documentation from authorized NVIDIA channels - the provenance chain a compliance team, auditor, or federal contracting officer can verify.

Consumer GPUs - RTX 4090s, 5090s, and their equivalents - deliver strong inference performance per dollar for personal projects and unregulated workloads. But without ECC memory, server-grade cooling, certified drivers, or a documented supply chain, they are the wrong foundation for a system that has to be trusted, audited, and run around the clock.

Upgrade Path

GPU Upgrade Program

As newer models demand more VRAM or faster memory bandwidth, Island Mountain offers a GPU upgrade path. This is a hardware swap - your existing GPUs are removed, credited at current secondary market value, and replaced with the next-generation cards.

This is not a firmware update or a software toggle. It's a physical component replacement that requires shipping the system or scheduling an on-site visit. Upgrade pricing depends on the current market price of the replacement GPUs minus your trade-in credit.

We disclose this upfront because "upgrade-ready" language in this industry often hides the reality: getting to the next tier of performance means buying new silicon. We'd rather you know that going in.

Common Questions

Product Questions

Can I upgrade from Summit Base to Summit Ridge later?

Yes. Your existing RTX PRO 6000 GPUs are credited at current secondary market value and replaced with higher-performance GPUs if available. This is a physical hardware swap, not a software update. The chassis, CPU, RAM, and storage remain the same. Upgrade pricing depends on the replacement GPU market price minus your trade-in credit. Turnaround is typically 2-4 weeks from order.

What is the largest model I can run on the Summit Base tier?

The Summit Base tier (192GB total VRAM) runs any model that fits within 192GB at FP16 precision, including all 70B-parameter models like Llama 4 Scout and DeepSeek R1 70B Distill. It runs DeepSeek V4-Flash at FP8 (about 170GB) with roughly 22GB of headroom. For V4-Flash at full FP16 quality, the Summit Pinnacle tier with 4x RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell GPUs (384GB VRAM) is the configuration to choose.

Are the GPUs new or used?

New. Summit Base and Summit Ridge tiers use new NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell Server Edition GPUs purchased through authorized NVIDIA channels with full procurement documentation. These are professional-grade server cards with ECC GDDR7 memory. Pricing is $59,000-$69,000 for Summit Base and $95,000-$120,000 for Summit Ridge. Every GPU is individually tested before assembly and then undergoes 72 hours of continuous burn-in testing. GPUs showing any instability are rejected.

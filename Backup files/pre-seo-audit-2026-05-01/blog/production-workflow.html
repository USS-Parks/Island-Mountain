Weekly Blog Production Workflow

Target: One publication-ready article per week. Total time: 90 minutes or less.

The 90-Minute Process

10 minutes

Step 1 — Pull the Week's Assignment

Open the 52-week editorial calendar. Note the article title, target keyword, buyer segment, and required internal links for the current week.

Run a quick search for the target keyword. Open the top 2-3 results. Note what angle they take, what depth they reach, and where they stop. Your article must go deeper or take a different angle. Never write the same article someone else already published.

Check the Island Mountain FAQ page. If the FAQ covers your topic, note exactly where the FAQ answer ends. Your article starts where the FAQ stops.

15 minutes

Step 2 — Outline

Write the skeleton before you write the article. This is the investment that makes drafting fast.

  • H1: The article title (from the calendar)
  • Intro paragraph: The specific problem this article solves, stated in 2-3 sentences
  • H2 sections: 4-6 sections with specific headers (not "Understanding the Technology" — specific: "Why MoE Architecture Changes the VRAM Calculation")
  • Key facts: Note at least 3 specific, verifiable numbers or claims you'll include
  • Internal links: Note the 2+ existing site pages you'll link to and where in the article they fit
  • Conclusion + CTA: One paragraph, 100-150 words, with a soft link to contact.html
45 minutes

Step 3 — Draft

Write to the outline. When you know the material — and you know this material — drafting is fast. When you need to look something up, look it up. Do not guess. Accuracy over speed, always.

Target: 1,200-1,800 words. If you're under 1,200, the article isn't going deep enough. If you're over 1,800, you're either covering two topics or padding.

Use the AI-assisted drafting prompt (included below) to generate a first draft if needed. Edit the output for voice and accuracy. Never publish an AI draft without a full human review pass.

10 minutes

Step 4 — Technical Accuracy Review

Read every technical claim. Verify every number against the live site or a source you trust.

  • No reference to A100 GPUs as a current Island Mountain product
  • No reference to DeepSeek R1 70B as a pre-installed model
  • Starter pricing: $75,000-$85,000 (not $60K-$70K)
  • Performance pricing: $150,000-$160,000
  • Premium pricing: $350,000-$400,000
  • DeepSeek V4-Flash described as quantized on Starter/Performance, near-FP16 on Premium
  • H100 Starter described as refurbished; Performance as build-to-order
  • No compliance claims without qualification
  • Scan for banned words (see full list in drafting prompt below)
10 minutes

Step 5 — Format and Publish

Copy the article body into blog-post-template.html. Save as a new file in the blog/ directory with a URL-friendly filename.

Fill all template placeholders:

  • [TITLE] — Article title
  • [META-DESCRIPTION] — 155 characters max, include the target keyword
  • [CANONICAL-URL] — Full URL: https://islandmountain.io/blog/filename.html
  • [PUBLISH-DATE] — Publication date
  • [CATEGORY] and [TAG-CLASS] — Category name and CSS class (tag-technical, tag-compliance, tag-financial, tag-how-to, tag-industry)
  • [READ-TIME] — Word count divided by 200, rounded up
  • [ARTICLE-SCHEMA-JSON] — BlogPosting JSON-LD (template provided in schema blocks document)
  • [RELATED-1/2/3-*] — Three related article links, titles, and excerpts

Add the new article card to blog.html at the top of the blog grid.

Add the article to sitemap.xml with changefreq: monthly and priority: 0.7.

AI-Assisted Drafting Prompt (Reusable)

Copy and paste this prompt into Claude or any capable LLM to generate a first draft. Edit the output for voice, accuracy, and completeness before publishing.

You are writing a blog article for islandmountain.io, a boutique local AI inference hardware company based in Colorado. The company is founded and operated by John Dougherty, a 25-year enterprise security and technology veteran.

ARTICLE DETAILS:
- Title: [INSERT TITLE]
- Target keyword: [INSERT KEYWORD]
- Category: [INSERT CATEGORY]
- Buyer segment: [INSERT BUYER SEGMENT]
- Required internal links: [INSERT PAGES TO LINK TO]

VOICE:
Write as if John Dougherty wrote it. Direct. Technically specific. Numbers, not adjectives. Dry, not playful. The humor is in the precision, not in jokes. First-person "we" where appropriate. No hedging when facts are clear. Hedge only when genuine uncertainty exists. Honest about limitations.

CURRENT HARDWARE CONFIGURATION (verify all claims against this):
- Starter: $75,000-$85,000. 2x NVIDIA H100 80GB PCIe (refurbished). 160GB combined VRAM. Ships now, pre-made.
- Performance: $150,000-$160,000. 2x NVIDIA H100 80GB (build-to-order, custom). 160GB combined VRAM. By deposit, 4-6 weeks.
- Premium: $350,000-$400,000. 2x NVIDIA H200 141GB. 282GB combined VRAM. Coming Q3 2026.
- Pre-installed models: DeepSeek V4-Flash (quantized, 284B params, ~13B active MoE, 1M context, MIT), Llama 3.1 70B (MIT), Mixtral 8x22B (Apache 2.0).
- Software: vLLM (tensor parallelism), Ollama, OpenWebUI, Ubuntu Server LTS.
- H100: HBM3, 3.35 TB/s bandwidth, 989 TFLOPS FP16.
- H200: HBM3e, 4.8 TB/s bandwidth, 989 TFLOPS FP16.

WHAT NO LONGER EXISTS (do not reference as current products):
- A100 GPUs
- DeepSeek R1 70B
- $60K-$70K Starter pricing

BANNED WORDS (hard stop, no exceptions):
delve, realm, harness, tapestry, paradigm, revolutionize, landscape, intricate, pivotal, surpass, meticulously, unparalleled, underscore, leverage, synergy, innovative, game-changer, testament, commendable, highlight, emphasize, groundbreaking, align, foster, showcase, enhance, holistic, garner, pioneering, trailblazing, unleash, versatile, transformative, redefine, seamless, optimize, scalable, robust, breakthrough, empower, streamline, frictionless, elevate, adaptive, effortless, data-driven, proactive, visionary, disruptive, reimagine, agile, unprecedented, intuitive, future-proof, AI-powered, results-driven.

COMPLIANCE LANGUAGE:
Never: "Island Mountain hardware is HIPAA compliant."
Always: "Island Mountain hardware eliminates the third-party transmission vector that creates HIPAA exposure."
Never: "Local AI complies with ITAR."
Always: "Local AI removes the cloud processing variable that creates ITAR risk for CUI-adjacent data."

STRUCTURE:
- Word count: 1,200-1,800 words
- Use H2 headers for sections (4-6 sections). Headers must be specific, not vague.
- At least 3 specific, verifiable facts or numbers
- At least 2 internal links to existing site pages
- Do NOT duplicate content the FAQ page already covers at the same depth. Go deeper.
- Conclusion paragraph: 100-150 words with a soft CTA linking to the contact page. Not salesy. Logical next step.
- Do not fabricate customers, case studies, or deployment numbers.

Write the article now.

Content Update Policy

When Published Articles Need Updating

Published articles become inaccurate when the underlying facts change. The following events trigger a mandatory review of all affected articles:

  • New model releases: Any new open-source model that changes VRAM tables, capability comparisons, or pre-installed model lists. Example: If DeepSeek releases V4-Pro in a locally-runnable configuration, every article referencing V4-Flash as the flagship model needs review.
  • Tier pricing changes: Any change to Starter, Performance, or Premium pricing. The Starter tier moved from $60K-$70K to $75K-$85K between versions of the site. Every article referencing pricing must be updated within 48 hours of a price change.
  • Hardware configuration changes: GPU swaps (the A100-to-H100 transition is exactly this scenario), VRAM changes, new tier additions, or tier removals. Every technical article must reflect current hardware.
  • Regulatory guidance changes: New bar ethics opinions on AI, HIPAA OCR guidance on AI use, ITAR/CMMC updates, new state privacy laws affecting AI data processing. Compliance articles must be reviewed within 30 days of any relevant regulatory publication.
  • Software stack changes: If the inference engine changes from vLLM to something else, if OpenWebUI is replaced, or if the OS changes. Technical and How-To articles must reflect the current stack.

Review Cadence

  • Technical articles: Review quarterly. Check all hardware specs, model names, VRAM figures, and performance claims against the live site.
  • Compliance articles: Review after any relevant regulatory guidance release, and at minimum every six months.
  • Financial articles: Review annually or when pricing changes. Check that TCO comparisons, financing rates, and tax guidance reflect current conditions.
  • How-To articles: Review when the software stack changes. OpenWebUI interface changes, vLLM configuration changes, or Ollama updates can make step-by-step guides inaccurate.
  • Industry articles: Review annually. Check that vertical-specific claims (tribal sovereignty frameworks, HIPAA mechanics, ITAR requirements) remain current.

Update Process

  • Update the article body content
  • Update the dateModified field in the article's JSON-LD schema
  • Update the lastmod date in sitemap.xml
  • If the update changes the article's core thesis or title, update the blog.html card excerpt
  • Do not change the article URL. Redirect if a title change makes the old URL misleading.

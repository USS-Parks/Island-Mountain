Compliance Dashboard

Seven operator views. Audit trails, policy enforcement, runtime monitoring, air-gap verification, and trust manifold status - all in one console. No data leaves your hardware.

The Lamprey MAI Compliance Dashboard is an operator console that ships with every Island Mountain server. It reads live data from the MAI compliance and trust APIs - the dashboard itself stores nothing. What you see below is an interactive preview showing the seven views your compliance team will use to monitor, audit, and enforce policy on your local AI infrastructure.

Core views (Overview, Audit, Reports, Alerts, Health) are functional in the current build. The Monitoring and Policy views are in active development, with additional panels and industry-specific templates expanding through Q3 2026.

Capabilities

What the Compliance Dashboard Covers

Tamper-evident audit trails. Every compliance decision - allow, deny, or flag - is recorded with cryptographic verification. The audit chain is append-only and verifiable on every read. Search by module, decision type, tenant, or time range.

Industry-specific policy templates. Apply pre-configured compliance profiles for healthcare (HIPAA audit controls), defense (ITAR/CMMC data residency), or tribal government (OCAP data sovereignty). Each template activates the right modules at the right priority. Financial services and energy/utilities templates are in development.

Air-gap verification. The monitoring console confirms your server's network isolation status in real time. Power state, connectivity posture, cloud route blocking, and local-only enforcement are all visible in a single panel. If your air gap breaks, the dashboard shows it before anything leaks.

Stateless architecture. The dashboard stores nothing locally. It reads from the MAI compliance and trust APIs on every page load. This means your compliance data has exactly one source of truth: the server itself. No sync issues, no stale caches, no data living where it shouldn't.

Prometheus-compatible metrics. Fourteen metric families are exposed for integration with existing monitoring infrastructure. GPU utilization, inference latency, scheduler queue depth, compliance decision counts, and trust claim status are all available as standard Prometheus exposition format.

The Market

The Market Nobody Is Serving

Cloud AI has a hard legal wall problem. HIPAA. ITAR. Attorney-client privilege. Tribal data sovereignty frameworks - OCAP principles, tribal gaming compact restrictions, sovereign government data governance mandates. These aren't preferences an organization can waive. They're legal prohibitions with criminal penalties for non-compliance. Every query sent to OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, or any cloud inference provider by an entity in these verticals is a compliance event waiting to become a liability.

The organizations that need AI most are the ones the cloud AI market cannot legally serve.

Enterprise vendors solve this for Fortune 500 companies at $1M and up. DIY builders offer no warranty, no burn-in, no compliance documentation, no support. The $59K to $225K mid-market - tribal governments, regional law firms, independent medical groups, mid-tier defense contractors - has no turnkey solution. That's the gap Island Mountain was built to occupy, and it's not a small gap. There are 574 federally recognized tribal nations in the United States alone. There are over 400,000 law firms. There are 6,100 hospitals and tens of thousands of independent medical practices. Every single one of them is watching their competitors deploy AI and asking the same question: how do we do this without violating our compliance obligations?

The answer they need is a rack in their building. We build that rack.

The Platform

Lamprey MAI: The Platform Thesis

Hardware is the entry point. Lamprey MAI is the reason this company compounds.

Every Island Mountain unit - Summit enterprise rack and Landfall household server - ships with Lamprey MAI, our proprietary local interface layer. In its current form, Lamprey MAI is a localized AI operating environment: curated system prompts, private model management, a clean family- and organization-grade interface running entirely on hardware you own. No data leaves the machine. No query is logged externally. No third party ever processes what runs through it.

That's the foundation. What it's becoming is larger.

Lamprey MAI is being built as the iOS of sovereign AI - a local operating system that sits between the user and the model, manages the full stack of AI interactions across an organization or household, and evolves through local update packages rather than cloud dependency. The hardware sale seeds the distribution. Lamprey MAI is what deepens the relationship, generates recurring update revenue, and builds the defensible platform moat that pure hardware companies never achieve.

When Apple sold the first iPhone, they were selling hardware. What they were building was a platform that would define a trillion-dollar ecosystem for the next two decades. Island Mountain's hardware is the device. Lamprey MAI is the operating system. The regulated industry and consumer privacy markets are the ecosystem.

COGS Breakdown

GPUs are about 50% of total cost, down from 78% on the prior refurbished-H100 build. At roughly $18,000 for two new professional Blackwell cards, total COGS drops from $84,278 to $36,278 - and the unit no longer hinges on a volatile secondary GPU market.

The Margin Thesis

Moving to professional Blackwell cards cuts GPU cost from roughly $66,000 to about $18,000 per unit. Total COGS falls from $84,278 to $36,278. That lets us lower the Summit Base price from $75-85K to $59-69K while expanding gross margin from break-even to 38-47%. The margin no longer depends on timing a volatile refurbished-GPU market - it comes from sourcing new, warrantied cards at professional-tier pricing through authorized channels.

Prior 2x H100 refurb build (COGS $84,278, ASP $75-85K) -12% to +1%
New 2x RTX PRO 6000 build (COGS $36,278, ASP $59-69K) 38-47%

The prior refurbished-H100 build sat at break-even and depended on locking scarce GPU supply at the right moment. The Blackwell build is structurally different: new cards at professional-tier pricing deliver a durable 38-47% gross margin even at a lower customer price. The business no longer needs a GPU-arbitrage window to make money - the margin is built into the bill of materials.

Risk Disclosure

What Could Go Wrong

Investors deserve founders who name their own risks. Here are ours.

Pre-Revenue Status

Zero revenue. No shipped units. This round funds the transition from development to market delivery. First unit target is 10 weeks post-close.

GPU Price Volatility

If RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell supply tightens or list prices rise, component costs increase. Mitigation: Price-lock clauses with buyers, 14-day quote windows, and a working-capital reserve for opportunistic component purchasing. Because the Blackwell build carries a 38-47% gross margin, a moderate GPU price increase compresses margin without pushing the unit underwater.

Dual-Track Execution

Running Summit enterprise and Landfall consumer simultaneously requires rigid operational focus to prevent resource fragmentation. Mitigation: Summit is the revenue priority. Landfall operates on pre-order deposits that fund their own build queue.

Lamprey MAI Development Risk

Software is in final validation. Interface layer and system prompt architecture in testing now. Mitigation: Hardware ships with Lamprey MAI at current capability; the platform evolves in parallel with commercial operations, not as a prerequisite to them.

Early-Stage Team

Two co-founders. Hardware builds depend on John; sales pipeline depends on Basho. Mitigation: First hire is an assembly technician. SOPs documented alongside first builds. Neither founder is a single point of failure for survival.

Competitive Entry

Dell, HPE, or a funded startup could enter this exact price range. Mitigation: Speed to market. Supplier relationships are the moat. In regulated verticals, customer trust and compliance documentation track record matter more than brand recognition.
